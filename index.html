What Is Online Personal Training?

Online personal training is a personalized coaching experience delivered through a digital platform instead of meeting in a gym. You receive a custom training plan designed specifically for your goals, fitness level, and available equipment — and you stay connected with your coach through regular check-ins, progress tracking, and exercise feedback.

1.

Fitness Assessment

Every client begins with a detailed online intake and movement assessment. This helps identify mobility restrictions, muscular imbalances, and training history so your program can be built around your body and goals.

2.

Customized Training Plan

Based on your assessment, you receive a structured training program designed specifically for you. Your plan includes:

  • Step-by-step workouts
  • Exercise video demonstrations
  • Sets, reps, tempo, and rest guidelines
  • Progressive programming to ensure continued improvement
3.

Train on Your Schedule

Workouts are delivered through an easy-to-use training app so you can train whenever it fits your schedule. Whether you're at home or in the gym, your program travels with you.

4.

Weekly Coaching & Feedback

Each week you'll check in with your coach to review your progress, ask questions, and receive feedback on exercise technique. Clients can upload videos of their exercises to receive coaching and corrections.

5.

Program Updates

Your training plan evolves as you improve. Programs are adjusted regularly to keep you progressing while reducing risk of injury.

About Aven Fitness

AvenFitness was created to provide structured, personalized fitness coaching for people who want real results without confusion, guesswork, or generic workout plans.

Many people struggle with fitness not because they lack effort, but because they lack a clear plan. AvenFitness was built to solve that problem by providing individualized training programs designed around each client's goals, movement patterns, and experience level.

Every program is developed using evidence-based training principles, including the NASM Optimum Performance Training (OPT™) model and corrective exercise strategies. This approach focuses on building a strong foundation of movement quality, strength, and long-term physical health.
